
● Great question. Enterprise companies handle this in a few
  ways, ranked by deliverability impact:

  1. Hosted image URL (best approach)
  Reference your logo as a regular <img> tag pointing to a
  publicly hosted URL on your own domain. This is what
  Postmark themselves recommend.

  <img src="https://bidapp.sa/logo.png" alt="Bid" />

  - No attachments, no base64 bloat, no spam triggers
  - Email clients fetch it on open (most modern clients do
  this)
  - You can update the logo without resending emails

  2. What to avoid:
  - Inline base64 — bloats email size, many clients strip
  it, hurts deliverability
  - CID attachments — adds MIME parts, increases spam score,
   inconsistent rendering
  - SVG — most email clients block SVG entirely

  Your current setup already does option 1 in spirit — the
  buildEmailHtml template renders a CSS-styled "B" box as
  the logo (<span style="...">B</span>). But you want the
  actual Bid logo image instead.

  What you need to do:
  1. Host the logo PNG on your domain (e.g.,
  https://bidapp.sa/logo.png) — keep it small (under 20KB,
  ~200px wide)
  2. Replace the text-based logo block in the email template
   with an <img> tag pointing to that URL
  3. Always include alt="Bid" as fallback for clients that
  block images by default

  - Low complaint rate — only email users who expect it
  - Consistent sending volume — don't spike from 0 to 1000 emails
