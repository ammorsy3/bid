Context & Rationale
We’re moving Bid from a role-based user model (Vendor vs. Requester) to a company-centric model where every registered entity represents a company that can act as both buyer and supplier.
The goal is to make the platform more natural to real-world business behavior — companies don’t fit in one box. The same organization can publish tenders and submit proposals. To support that, we’ll unify everything under a single Company Account model.
Why We’re Changing It
Real-world alignment: In practice, companies alternate between requesting and supplying services. One identity, one verification, one profile makes that flow seamless.
Data consistency: Legal, compliance, and profile information belong to the company, not to the individual user.
Collaboration & scaling: We’ll enable each company to invite team members (users) with different permissions — owner, admin, viewer, etc.
Flexibility for consultants and multi-business users: A single user might belong to multiple companies (for example, managing several CRs). This will later allow cross-organization work without creating separate accounts.
Simpler verification: One Pre-Qualification and one Verification status per company, reused in all contexts.
Unified analytics: All actions (creating tenders, submitting proposals) are tracked under one entity, so we can analyze company performance as a whole.
Expected Structural Changes
Introduce a Company entity as a top-level object, separate from users.
Replace the one-to-one relationship between user and role with a many-to-many relationship between users and companies through a linking table (user_companies).
Add a role_in_company field to define access levels (Owner, Admin, Member, Viewer).
Store pre-qualification, verification status, and public profile under the company record instead of per user.
The platform context will switch dynamically based on the active company a user is operating within.
Functional Impact
Registration flow: A new user either creates a company or joins an existing one.
Dashboard: All actions are contextual to the “active company.”
Profile & Pre-Qualification: Shared company-wide. Once verified, all members inherit the verified status.
Invitations: Requesters can invite entire companies (not individuals).
Roles & permissions: Admins can add, remove, or manage team members inside their company account.
Future readiness: Enables white-label, multi-organization features, agency accounts, and shared analytics dashboards later on.
In summary
We’re shifting from user-role identity to a company-entity model.
This change simplifies onboarding, supports team collaboration, prevents data duplication, and mirrors how businesses actually operate. It will require adjustments to authentication, permissions, data ownership, and the onboarding journey — but it will make Bid much more scalable and enterprise-ready.
