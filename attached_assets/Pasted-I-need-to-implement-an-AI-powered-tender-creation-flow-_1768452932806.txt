I need to implement an AI-powered tender creation flow called "Bid Copilot" in my app. This will be a new route at /tenders/new/use-ai (or suggest a better path name).

## Overview
Create a chat-first interface where users describe what they need, and AI builds a complete tender through an intelligent conversation, asking follow-up questions and suggesting requirements based on the service type.

## UI Layout (see attached wireframe)
- Split-screen design inspired by Lemlist
- Left sidebar: Progress tracker showing 5 steps (Company → Service → Details → Prefs → Review) with checkmarks for completed steps, plus a "Tip Helper" box at the bottom
- Main area: Chat interface on the left side, live tender preview building on the right side as the conversation progresses
- Top: "Bid Copilot" header with close button
- Bottom right: Back and Continue buttons

## Step-by-Step Flow

**Step 1: Company Context**
- Auto-fetch and display company data from the database (name, bio, website, category, city, tags, logo)
- Show this pre-filled information in the chat
- Ask user: "I've pulled your company information. Does everything look correct, or would you like to update anything?"
- Allow inline editing if needed

**Step 2: Service/Product Needed**
- Prompt: "What service or product are you looking to procure? Describe it in your own words."
- User types freely in natural language (e.g., "I need office cleaning services for 3 buildings")
- AI processes this and moves to next step

**Step 3: Requirements (AI goes deep here)**
- Based on the service type from Step 2, AI should:
  - Determine project type (ongoing service, one-time purchase, project-based, etc.)
  - Suggest budget ranges appropriate for this service type
  - Suggest timeline/duration
  - Ask about quantity, scope, location requirements
  - Suggest specific deliverables common to this service type
  - Suggest quality standards, certifications, or compliance requirements
  - Ask contextual follow-up questions
- AI should be proactive and suggest concrete requirements, not just ask open-ended questions
- User can accept, modify, or reject suggestions

**Step 4: Preferences**
- AI suggests vendor preferences if applicable
- Ask about required certifications
- **Critical: Ask for submission deadline** (this is the one thing AI cannot guess - user must provide)
- Any other procurement-specific preferences

**Step 5: Review & Generate**
- Show complete tender in structured format
- Allow final edits
- "Publish" or "Save Draft" buttons
